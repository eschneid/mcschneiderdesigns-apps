Hand and Foot is a Canasta-variant card game for 2 or more players. Build melds of matching cards, exhaust both your Hand and Foot piles, and rack up the highest score.

How to Play

Each player is dealt two piles: a Hand (played first) and a Foot (picked up when the Hand is exhausted). On your turn, draw two cards, then meld sets of 3 or more matching cards into books. Go out by emptying both piles while your team has the required number of complete books.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Clean Book?

A Clean Book (Red Book) is a complete set of 7 natural cards with no wild cards. Clean books are worth more points and are required to go out.

What is a Dirty Book?

A Dirty Book (Black Book) is a complete set of 7 cards that includes at least one wild card (2 or Joker). Dirty books are worth fewer points than clean books but are easier to complete.

What are wild cards?

2s and Jokers are wild and can substitute for any natural card in a meld. A meld cannot contain more wild cards than natural cards.

What does Going Out mean?

Going out means you have played all cards from both your Hand and Foot piles and your team has the required complete books. Going out earns a bonus and ends the round.

How do I remove ads?
