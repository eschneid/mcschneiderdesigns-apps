Euchre is a fast-paced 4-player partnership trick-taking game played with a 24-card deck. Play as North/South against East/West, or pass the device between friends in pass-and-play mode.

How to Play

Each round a trump suit is chosen and the calling team must win at least 3 of 5 tricks. Score 1 point for making your bid, 2 for a march (all 5 tricks), or 2 if you euchre the opponents. First team to 10 points wins.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Euchre?

If the team that called trump fails to win at least 3 tricks, they are “euchred” and the opposing team scores 2 points. Calling trump without the cards to back it up is a big risk.

What does Going Alone mean?

A player can choose to go alone, sending their partner out of the round. Win all 5 tricks alone and your team scores 4 points instead of 2 for a march.

What is the Bower rule?

In Euchre, the Jack of the trump suit (Right Bower) is the highest card, followed by the Jack of the same-color suit (Left Bower) as the second-highest trump. For example, if Hearts is trump, J♥ ranks highest and J♦ ranks second.

Why do I see 6 and 4 cards on the table?

The 6 and 4 of each suit are used as traditional score-keeping cards. The 4-card slides upward as your team’s score increases, revealing more of the 6 beneath it — a classic Euchre score-tracking method.
